# Species

## Overview

The facility cultivates microalgae species selected for high-value compound production. The primary production species is *Haematococcus pluvialis* for natural astaxanthin.

## Haematococcus pluvialis

*Haematococcus pluvialis* is a freshwater green microalga known for producing the carotenoid astaxanthin under stress conditions. It is the primary production species at the facility.

### Life Cycle Stages

The life cycle of *H. pluvialis* consists of four morphological stages:

![Haematococcus pluvialis Life Cycle](../images/haematococcus_lifecycle.svg)
*Four morphological stages of H. pluvialis from vegetative to cyst.*

1. **Vegetative (green) stage** — Motile, biflagellate cells actively dividing. Cells are green due to chlorophyll dominance.
2. **Palmella stage** — Loss of flagella, cells become non-motile and enlarge. Transitional stage.
3. **Early cyst (encystment)** — Cell wall thickening begins. Astaxanthin accumulation starts, cells turn orange-red.
4. **Mature cyst (aplanospore)** — Fully encysted, thick-walled resting cells. Maximum astaxanthin content (~5% dry weight). Cells are deep red.

### Cultivation Parameters

| Parameter | Green Stage | Red Stage |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Nitrogen | Sufficient | Depleted |
| Light | Moderate | High |
| Temperature | 20-25 degC | 20-25 degC |
| pH | 7.0-7.5 | 7.0-7.5 |
