# Medium

## Overview

Growth medium is prepared centrally in the CFU (Central Feed Unit) and distributed to individual MPC reactors via the feed system. Medium composition is tailored to the cultivation phase and species requirements.

## Preparation

### CFU Process

The CFU prepares sterile nutrient medium from raw water:

1. **Water intake** — Raw water from municipal supply
2. **Filtration** — Pre-filtration to remove particulates
3. **Nutrient dosing** — Macro and micronutrients added per recipe
4. **Sterilization** — Heat or UV treatment
5. **Storage** — Prepared medium held in IBC containers or feed tanks
6. **Distribution** — Pumped to MPC reactors via feed manifold

### Nutrient Components

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| Nitrogen (N) | Cell growth, protein synthesis |
| Phosphorus (P) | Energy transfer, DNA/RNA |
| Iron (Fe) | Photosynthesis, electron transport |
| Magnesium (Mg) | Chlorophyll core |
| Trace metals | Enzyme cofactors |

## Feed Control

Medium is delivered to reactors through the CFU feed system. Each MPC controls feed valve timing and volume per reactor based on growth phase requirements.

## Gaps

- Exact medium recipe (concentrations per nutrient)
- Water quality specifications (conductivity, pH, hardness)
- Sterilization method and parameters (temperature, duration, UV dose)
- Feed rate and frequency per growth phase
- IBC container management (cleaning, reuse cycles)
- Medium shelf life after preparation
